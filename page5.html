Julia II.

When we asked Julia II to write her story down concerning depression we were not quite prepared of the depth of her feelings. What stood out for us was that she mentioned that depression is next to a mental pain also something very physical, we weren't quite expecting that and it made us think in a different direction. We decided to make the experiments into a physical experience and therefore put the human body into focus. By putting the body in focus, we wanted to emphasize the physical suffering. Julia described a feeling of getting small and closed in.

The charcoal drawing is symbolizing the ‘closing in’. The drawing is making her surrounding smaller and smaller and at the end it swallows her. The sound of the charcoal became for us a very important feature, we felt it symbolized the discomfort, anxiety and fear that comes with a depression.
