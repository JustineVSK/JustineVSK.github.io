Projects

“Depression is like an uninvited guest at a party...”, that’s how depression was described by Olga. We asked her and others to write down for us how they experienced depression. We discovered that depression is a subject that is sensitive and that not everyone is comfortable with speaking about it. When they explain their thoughts, they do this in their own way: one uses keywords, another visualizes it through an image, one person only feels comfortable by having a personal conversation or others would like to write it down in a mail or a handwritten letter. 

 




  



01
Julia I


“... I am locked in a glass box, the walls are coming closer and closer. I am stuck inside, but I can see the outside world through the transparent walls. We can see each other, but we can not hear and understand each other ".

 

 

 



02
Julia II


“I become small and the magnificence of nature feels overwhelming. The body hurts, I experience headaches, back pain and fatigue. I feel locked in my own body and can not escape my negative thoughts…”

  

 

 




03
Virginie


“...Drowning in your own fears until you lose any vitality. Your body shuts down but your mind is racing with a billion thoughts creating anxiety, phobias, physical fear and trembling. There is nothing you can do, but deep diving in your most irrational fear, self judgement, expectation and anger.

 

 

 




04
Willemijn


“…Depression means loneliness. You find yourself in a negative spiral and you increasingly fall deeper, it is becoming harder to get out. The meaning of life has left, fear exists that there is no way out. The world is asleep and you are the only one awake, while everyone else is resting you are fighting against yourself, your own thoughts.”

 

 

 



05
Luna


I used to be a different Luna each day, because I am afraid of showing myself, who I really am. I would be loud, funny Luna in the morning and changing in to silent Luna at night, it was like I as playing a puppet show…”

  

  

 



06
Olga


“...Fear that suddenly comes to mind, like an uninvited guest at a party, a strange feeling of being very restless in your body and you want to get away from it. Run as fast as you can to get rid of that restless and threatening feeling, but it follows you around.."
